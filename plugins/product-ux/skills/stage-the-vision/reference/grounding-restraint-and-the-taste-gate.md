# Grounding, restraint & the taste gate

The floor the artistic engine stands on. Inventing bold moments without this becomes garish, trendy, or self-indulgent — the *other* failure. Grounding, restraint, and an outside eye keep the art honest. This is discipline on the creative act, not a replacement for it: it removes the bad so the bold can read as *good*.

## Ground it in reality (so it's not generic)

Boldness invented from a model's priors drifts to trends. Anchor it:

- **Use whatever real material exists.** *If* the project has a brand, prior work, or imagery, that is the strongest anchor — adopt it, stay continuous with it, never invent a louder version because it "sings." Many projects have none; that's normal — then ground in real touchstones gathered with the user (and the great work you're adapting, `stealing-from-great-work.md`). An existing identity is a bonus to honor when present, **never a prerequisite**.
- **Where real imagery exists, sample the palette from it**; otherwise derive a small palette from the references and confirm it. A palette drawn from reality is coherent by construction.

## Restraint — bold ≠ loud

The single discipline that keeps "make it striking" from becoming "make it garish":

- **One concept, executed with control.** The *idea* is daring; the *rendering* is restrained. A striking screen is usually quiet around one strong move, not loud everywhere.
- **Limited palette, contrast budget.** A small set; spend contrast deliberately (text legibility, one focal accent) and let the rest rest. "Vivid" is not a goal; intensity is not impact. Garish is everything shouting at once.
- **One dominant move per screen.** If two effects compete, cut one. Spectacle for its own sake is the card-grid failure in a louder costume.
- **Earn every effect.** Each motion, gradient, 3D moment must serve the one feeling. Whimsy that serves the feeling is art; whimsy that serves itself is noise.
- **The generative-cliché check.** Mesh/aurora gradients, blobs, glassmorphism, breathing pools, frosted floating cards — interrogate these exactly like card grids. "Is this *this* project's, or is it 2021's?" Escaping one trend into another is not originality.

## The taste gate — an outside eye, judging for beauty

The most important defense, because the model's own taste is the thing that failed:

- **The model may not self-certify aesthetics.** Treat your own "this sings / this is stunning / this is cinematic" as **suspect by default** — the classic failure is a model certifying round after round while the user disagrees every time. You may verify *mechanics* (it renders, RTL, reduced-motion, keyboard); you may **not** be the judge of *is this good / beautiful / striking.*
- **Explore 2–3 distinct directions at LOW fidelity** — different concepts, different restraint levels, cheap (a key-frame sketch, a palette + type specimen, the hero moment described or roughed) — not one polished commitment. Comparison makes judgment possible; one option can only be rubber-stamped.
- **Let the outside eye choose, judging for *beauty and impact*** — the user's eye, or an honest comparison against the great work you studied. "Does this stop me? Is it beautiful? Is it art?" — not merely "is it not-generic."
- **Cheap, early, comparative beats polished, late, reactive.** Get the eye in *before* the high-fidelity build, on small comparable directions — not after, on a finished site that must be torn down.

## Confirm before building

The chosen, grounded, restrained Staging is **confirmed before the high-fidelity build** — `architecture-first` in the design layer. Then it goes to `realize-the-vision`. Never build the full thing and discover the art was wrong.

## Pitfalls

- **Bold becoming loud** — maximal effects, cranked saturation, spectacle chasing "striking." The garish failure.
- **Self-certified art** — shipping on the model's own "it's stunning," no outside eye, no comparison.
- **One polished commitment, iterated reactively** — full builds shown one at a time instead of cheap comparable directions judged early.
- **Trend-by-reflex** — reaching for the impressive default (organic/aurora/glass) and feeling creative.
- **Overriding an existing identity when one exists** — a louder look than the real one because it "sings." (When none exists, just don't invent one to override.)
- **Building high-fidelity before the Staging is confirmed** — architecture-first violated in the design layer.
